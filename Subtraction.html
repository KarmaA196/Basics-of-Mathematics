What is Subtraction?

Subtraction is taking away a number or amount from another number or amount. It is the inverse operation of addition. While addition adds or puts together numbers to find the sum, subtraction removes a number from another number to find the difference.

How to Solve One-Digit Subtraction
To subtract one-digit numbers, simply subtract starting from the ones place. If the top number is smaller than the bottom number, you need to borrow.
Example: 9 - 4
Step 1: Subtract the ones place (9 - 4).
Answer: 9 - 4 = 5

In this example, we are starting with 9 stars, and subtracting 4 stars.

When we do this, we get a difference of 5 stars.

How to Solve Two-Digit Subtraction
To subtract two-digit numbers, align the digits by place value and subtract starting from the ones place. If the digit on top is smaller than the one below, borrow 1 from the tens place.
Example: 42 - 19
Step 1: Subtract the ones place (2 - 9). Borrow 1 from the tens place.
Step 2: 2 becomes 12, and 12 - 9 equals 3.
Step 3: Subtract the tens place (4 - 1 = 3).
Answer: 42 - 19 = 23

How to Solve Three-Digit Subtraction
To solve three-digit subtraction, align the digits by place value. Subtract starting from the ones place, borrowing as needed.
Example: 725 - 489
Step 1: Subtract the ones place (5 - 9). Borrow 1 from the tens place.
Step 2: Subtract the tens place (1 - 8). Borrow from the hundreds place.
Step 3: Subtract the hundreds place (6 - 4 = 2).
Answer: 725 - 489 = 236
